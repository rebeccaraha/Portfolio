Piggy Bank Clay Model

DIY Design + Fabrication - Carnegie Mellon University | Fall 2017
Independent Project

Choosing & Editing CAD File

The original Piggy Bank file, shown to the right, was found on Grabcad. From there, the file was brought into Solidworks and modified to fill the center and refine the design to be more desirable.

Unfortunately, as I am not a master at CAD, the model wasn't able to be refined as much as desired. For this reason, the final clay model was made to reflect those desired changes though.

Refining & Completion

Initial Refinement

Unfortunately, all of the photos I took during the refinement process were damaged and unable to be recovered (even after hours of Googling and speaking with Applecare). For this reason, there are no other progress pictures besides the ones supplied here.

During this time, the legs were leveled multipled times, the sides were refined - both by adding and removng material - to make the pig symmetrical, and the clay was smoothed overall.

For the final model some creatively liberties were taken in order to craft the model more to my taste than I was able to do in Solidworks.
