Human-Centered Design Hack-a-thon

Social Innovation Club - Carnegie Mellon University | Fall 2016

Last Fall, I had the pleasure of being the 'Wildcard Facilitator' at Carnegie Mellon's first Human-Centered Design Hack-a-thon - hosted by the Heinz School's Social Innovation club.

The HCD Hack-a-thon was a five-hour hack-a-thon experience where interdisciplinary teams were paired with local Social Impact Initiativies in order to tackle their biggest barriers.

As the only Wildcard Facilitator, my role was to float between groups and ensure they were on track with the timeline and aid them in moving forward. I aided teams in assessing what Design Thinking activities would help move their process forward and helped facilitated difficult discussions.

The HCD Hack-a-thon enabled me to use seom Design Thinking activities in my wheel-house and practice facilitating groups without leading them in any certain diretion.
