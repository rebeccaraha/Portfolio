Volitant

DIY Design + Fabrication - Carnegie Mellon University | Fall 2017
Independent Project

Scope

Design a cup for Moody Judy's angtsy teenager clientele which keeps their focus on the shirt and not on their scladed hands.

Ergonomics Research

Understanding how people hold cups and how that translates to different cup-holder options.

Ideation

A selection of the ideas explored and sketching and detailing proposed design can be seen here. Once an idea was chosen, it was translated into Adobe Illustrator and possible patterns were explored. Despite the flair they would add to the design, patterns were not utilized due to structural integrity concerns once the cup-holder was in use. If thicker acrylic were to be used in the future, these designs would be revisited.

Fabrication

Fabrication prototyping began with a cardboard prototype to test the proposed design. After this test, the handle legnth was increased to provide more room for users with larger hands.

The first acrylic cup holder was formed with a heat gun around a small wheel to create a curved handle. This technique did not give the desired form, so another piece was formed against the side of the table to give the handle two sharper turns instead. After the two bends were created, the cup holder was crooked so the heat gun was used again to reform the acrylic properly.

When trying to reform the acrylic, I got a littler overzelaous and applied too much pressed at the rim of the cup holder. Another set of cupholders were cut for a second attempt at forming with the heat gun.

In the final photo, you can see the three main designs explored. This evolution shows the final cup, on the right, before it was reformed one last time to make the top and bottom cocentric, which can be seen below.
