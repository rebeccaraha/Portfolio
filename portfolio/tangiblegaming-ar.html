Tangible Gaming + Augmented Reality

Methods for Research Innovation - Carnegie Mellon University | Fall 2017
Team Members: Annisa Prasetyanto & Brandon Zepada

Scope

Our Tangible Gaming + Augmented Reality project began with a simple ask: explore the opportunities at the intersection of gaming and AR.

With that scope, our team of three worked nimbly and rapidly over two and a half weeks to explore the gaming and AR landscape and all of the opportunities that might exist. This all began with a question... well, a few...

What is the future of Tangible Gaming + Augmented Reality?

What games or industries are ripe for AR?

Is there an ideal demographic or game-type for AR integration?

Are old school gamers open to the idea of technological integration?

Does exisiting applications of AR integration help players to be more immersed in the game or is just a novelty?

How can AR improve tangible gaming expriences are opposed to diluting them?

What makes AR a true integration as opposed to a gimicky addition?

Futurist Wheel

From there we created a Futurist Wheel in order to explore our understanding of the present and future landscape of Tangible Gaming + Augmented Reality and create a mutual understanding of that the landscape may be.

Based on our Futurist Wheel we found many areas within Tangible Gaming and Augmented Reality to explore. As a team we decided to explore types of gaming before choosing an area to specify in - all beginning with research!

Research Timeline

Throughout the course of this research product, we have the opportunity to engage with many communities and conduct many different types of research. As a team we chose methods which would complement one another while uncovering deeper and different insights.


Gaming Landscape

From there, we explored the gaming landscape... and ultimately chose to focus on Tabletop Games due to their potential for deeper interactions through the integration of augmented reality and due to some tabletop games' cult-like followings.

Intial Interviews & Contextual Inquiry

To kick off the research itself and get a feel for the gaming landscape and gaming culture, Interviews and Contextual Inquiry were conducted with those active in the gaming community - both in- and outside of the greater Pittsburgh area.

Narrowing Scope

Through this process it was determined that Magic: The Gathering and Dungeons and Dragons were the best subjects for this study. They were chosen for two main reasons:

  • Having strong userbases with highly committed gameplayers
  • Complex gameplay that could be eased and augmented through Augmented Reality applications

Next Steps

After our first round of interviews & contextual inquiry, we narrowed scope and entered an intensive two-week research sprint compromised of more contextual inquiries, co-designs, artifact analysis, observation, and applied ethnographic research.

These complimentary research methods allowed us to create recommendations for the future of AR + tangible gaming.
